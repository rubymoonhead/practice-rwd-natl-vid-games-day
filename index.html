Will the real National Video Games Day please stand up

Playing video games is one of my favorite activities.
This is the reason I chose this special September holiday theme for this coding lab activity.

NVGD is celebrated yearly and only in this country.

There is also a "National Video Game Day" that's celebrated on 8 July; however, this site is dedicated to the real NVGD and that is September's National Video Games Day.

According to Calendarr, in 1997, the celebrated holiday's date moved from 8 July to 12 September, and the event was then named "National Video Games Day".

The Video Game History Foundation says that the first day NVGD was celebrated was on 8 July 1991, then the NVGD date moved to 12 September 1997.
The author goes on to say that a certain David Earle, President of Kid Vid Warriors, had sponsored the event every year from 8 July 1991 to 8 July 1993, then on 12 July 1994 and 12 July 1995, and finally on 10 September 1996.
The article shows printed proof (a 1997 edition of Chase's Calendar of Events) that 12 September is the official date for National Video Games Day.

TheGamer says that the 8 July National Video Game Day holiday is a "pointless holiday".
TheGamer believes that the only one true video games holiday is on 12 September and is National Video Games Day.
The 8 July holiday was, according to the author, "a made up holiday created by the fake president of a pretend club in a phoney book".
I laughed after reading that line.

I, too, believe that the only real celebrated National Video Games Day is on 12 September.
"National Video Games Day" looks and sounds a lot better than "National Video Game Day".
Besides, the "games" version gives me a good feeling that this holiday celebration includes every video game and every type of video game player.
